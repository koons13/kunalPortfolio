Process

1. Task System Design

Daily Tasks: Expired in 24h â†’ drove retention.

Seasonal Tasks: Available throughout the run â†’ provided progression goals.

Each task awarded tokens â†’ tokens unlocked milestones.

2. Reward Structure

Free Track: Base rewards for all players.

Premium Track: Exclusive rewards for pass purchasers.

Endless Rewards: Introduced after milestone 30 to keep late-stage players engaged.

3. Perks Design

Premium Pass included gameplay perks such as:

  • +20% Barn Space
  • Faster Crafting & Foraging
  • "Water Everything at Once" shortcut
  • Seasonal perks like "Divine Shower" (Christmas theme)

These perks improved the daily loop without breaking balance.

5. Theming & Integration

Seasonal runs (Christmas, Summer Fest) refreshed art and tasks.

Implemented in Unity, ensuring smooth live-ops integration.

4. Iteration & Experimentation

Battle Pass V2 ("Summer Fest"):
  • Increased ARPU by ~20% vs. control
  • Purchaser conversion improved by +250 bps vs. MVP
  • Completion rate for pass purchasers rose to 57%
  • VIP survey: 92% of players wanted to repurchase
Premium Battle Pass (v18):
  • Added a premium tier at $19.99 vs. $9.99 regular
  • Delivered +12.7% ARPU uplift
  • 73% of BP buyers chose Premium
  • Completion rate for Premium users: 71.7% (vs. 59% regular)
  • Repurchase rate: 75%, higher than previous runs
Experiments:
  • Tested hiding half of free rewards â†’ improved conversion but caused confusion (CS tickets)
  • Shifted premium rewards earlier to increase perceived value
  • Tuned task difficulty across level buckets to balance completion

Key Learnings

Design principles applied

01.

Progressive disclosure

Revealed complexity gradually to avoid overwhelming players with too many options at once.

02.

Clear value proposition

Made the benefits of both free and premium tiers immediately apparent to players.

03.

Achievable goals

Designed tasks that felt challenging but not impossible to maintain player motivation.

04.

Seamless integration

Ensured the Battle Pass felt like a natural part of the game rather than an add-on feature.

Key takeaways

This project taught me the importance of balancing player needs with business objectives in game design. The Battle Pass needed to feel rewarding for players while driving meaningful engagement and revenue.

Cross-functional collaboration was crucial for success. Working closely with Product, Game Design, Art, and Development teams ensured the feature was both technically feasible and aligned with the game's vision.

The iterative nature of game development taught me to design for flexibility and scalability, allowing the feature to evolve and improve over time based on player feedback and data insights.
