Discover

Maintaining systems is a struggle

During a user interview for a project, the participant was sharing their screen to walk me through their workflow. At one point, they needed to reference a book and opened their chat to self and searched there. When I asked how they usually manage their "to-be-read" list, they explained it was spread across different placesâ€” a notes app, Google Keep, occasionally Goodreads, and often WhatsApp.

This mix of tools highlighted how people rarely have a single, consistent system for organizing personal knowledge. It made me curious about the trade-offs they make between convenience and structure.

I decided to explore

Why are users maintaining multiple systems?

Research

Survey (N=34)

I conducted an online survey with 30 participants. I wanted to understand how people capture ideas, manage lists, and take notes across different contexts.

This reinforced the insight that fragmentation is normal. People choose the tool that feels most convenient in the moment, even if it means juggling multiple systems later.

Users prioritize convenience even if it leads to fragmentation

Competitor Analysis

I studied existing solutions and the gaps reported by the users on platforms like reddit, etc with focus on short hand note-taking.

"Obsidian is amazing... literally amazing... but if you want to have a life where you actually DO WORK, rather than FIDDLE WITH YOUR SYSTEMS... then don't use it. I only use Obsidian for its public notes feature now. It's dead to me otherwise. Trust me on this - I used to use Obsidian with 92 plugins installed. It's a dream to be out of Obsidian's configuration hell."

"Notion was beautiful at first, but eventually it became a maze. I switched to something barebones."

"Minimal apps win. After trying everything, I went back to the default notes app on my phone."

Interviews (N=8)

I conducted interviews with 8 participants with various backgrounds to verify the insights

"I've been using WhatsApp self-chat since 2017 â€” everything goes there: ideas, reminders, to-dos. I find the items later by scrolling to rough estimate of date entered."

"For academic notes, I use GoodNotes with color coding â€” red for gaps, blue for tangents. I set it up a while back, but I haven't kept up with it."

Interviews revealed a clear pattern: the more complex the system, the harder it was to maintain. Even with structured tools available, people defaulted to easier tools like WhatsApp for its simplicity.

Ease of data entry is inversely proportional to ease of data retrieval.
